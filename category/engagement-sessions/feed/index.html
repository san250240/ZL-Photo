A spring afternoon at the Arboretum

Spring finally showed up in Boston, and we took full advantage. The Arboretum was all green and just starting to bloom, those in-between weeks when everything feels fresh but not crowded yet. Beth and Matt wanted something low-key, nothing staged, so that’s exactly how we did it.

We started wandering the main path, talking about random stuff, wedding plans, favorite takeout spots. Every now and then we’d stop when the light hit right or when something caught our eyes.

The photos everyone loves are always the unplanned ones. Like Matt helping Beth swap her shoes after the outfit change, or the way they laughed when a dog ran straight through our shot and almost stole the show. Stuff you can’t script. That’s why I love sessions like this, they feel like hanging out with friends who just happen to be getting some killer photos at the same time.

Two outfits, one vibe

About halfway through, Beth switched outfits. It wasn’t a big production, she just ducked into the visitor center and came back in something a little dressier. And honestly, it changed the feel in the best way. We found this spot under the trees where the light filtered through, and it felt like we were in a different park entirely.

A Carson Beach Engagement Session with Kayla and Mike

We started the afternoon on Carson Beach with Kayla and Mike teasing each other about baseball, one in a Red Sox jersey, the other proudly repping the Yankees. That little rivalry set the tone for the whole first half of the session: playful and full of inside jokes. They wandered along the sand, kicked off their shoes, and before long, we were all laughing as they splashed in the water with their feet submerged in the ocean.

Playful Beach Photos to Kick Things Off

Carson Beach has the perfect mix of open space and ocean views, which makes it ideal for candid, natural engagement photos. Kayla and Mike were laughing before we even started shooting. They joked about their rival teams as we walked along the sand, stopping for photos here and there.

Eventually, they kicked off their shoes, wandered closer to the water, and ended up standing ankle-deep in the ocean. It’s moments like that—feet in the water, completely carefree—that make an engagement session feel personal.

A Quick Change for Elegant Sunset Shots

After we wrapped up the playful beach photos, Kayla and Mike changed into dressier outfits for the second half of their engagement session. Kayla looked stunning in a royal blue cocktail dress, and Mike kept it classic in a white polo and dress pants.

The vibe shifted instantly to calm, romantic, and a little more intimate. These photos had a completely different feel than the first half, which is exactly what makes mixing outfits so great.

Why Choose Carson Beach for Engagement Photos?

If you’re planning a Boston couples session, Carson Beach is a gem. It gives you that open, airy beach look without leaving the city, and Castle Island is just a short walk away if you want even more variety. From playful shots in the sand to elegant evening portraits, it’s a spot that really lets your personality come through.

Kayla and Mike’s engagement session shows that you don’t have to pick one vibe. Start with something casual that feels like you, then switch it up for a more classic look. It keeps the session fun, and you end up with a gallery that feels full and personal.
